About Natalie

Natalie Goh is a passionate Occupational Therapist with over 8 years of clinical experience across disability, rehabilitation, community health, mental health, and aged care settings.

She completed her Bachelor of Applied Science (Occupational Therapy) Honours Class I at University of Sydney in 2018. During her studies, she completed and published her honours thesis, Thriving in an expanding service landscape: Experiences of occupational therapists working in generic mental health roles within non-government organisations in Australia, which she presented at the National OTA Conference and local symposiums.

Natalie has experience working across disability, rehabilitation, aged care, and community settings, including within NDIS, My Aged Care, and with Sony Foundation disability camps. Her work has included functional assessments, rehabilitation, assistive technology, major and minor home modifications, and supporting individuals to improve independence and quality of life through person-centred care.

She further developed her clinical skills at a rehabilitation hospital across inpatient and outpatient rehabilitation programs, supporting individuals with neurological, orthopaedic, trauma, and oncology-related conditions.

Most recently, Natalie has worked as a Senior Occupational Therapist at a Special Needs School since 2022, supporting children and adolescents with Level 2 and 3 autism spectrum disorder to engage meaningfully in school, community, and everyday activities.

Her professional interests include autism and neurodevelopment, sensory regulation, rehabilitation, assistive technology, functional capacity assessment, and promoting independence across all stages of life.

Outside of work, she enjoys being out in nature, hiking and swimming. She also loves travelling and trying new foods with her family and friends.
